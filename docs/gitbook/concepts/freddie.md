# Freddie, the Workspace Steward

Freddie is the workspace's own agent — a second set of eyes on the record itself.

Where the colleagues on your [Agents](../apps/agents.md) roster do the work you ask for, Freddie tends the workspace: what's landed, how it's organised, what's arrived from your connections, and whether anything needs your attention.

> **Freddie is in beta.** He does reversible things — organising, deriving, noticing. He does not take consequential action on your behalf.

## You meet Freddie in the record, not in a chat window

There's deliberately no "ask Freddie" chat box. His presence is what he leaves behind: activity entries reading *"Freddie derived…"*, and the `freddie:` attribution on revisions he authored.

That's the design. A steward who has to be summoned isn't tending anything.

## His two dials

Both live at **Workspace Settings → System**.

### Autonomy — the witness dial

Autonomy sets **when** you see something, not how much freedom the system has. Three settings, described in the product as:

- **Goes ahead** — acts, and tells you afterwards
- **Asks you first** — proposes, and waits for you
- **Waits for more** — holds until there's enough to act on

The distinction matters: work is never blocked because the system doesn't trust itself. It's queued because *you* asked to see it before it binds.

### Budget — the spend envelope

Budget caps what the workspace can spend on AI work. Setting it lower means the steward paces itself; setting it to zero stops the draw.

See [Usage and spend controls](../plans/usage.md).

## What Freddie doesn't do

- He isn't on the Agents roster — he's the workspace's steward, not a colleague you hire
- He doesn't appear as a chat lane
- He doesn't take actions with consequences outside the workspace
