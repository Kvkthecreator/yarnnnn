## Structural Analysis

### What Claude does well

**Carousel as narrative arc**: Each slide builds a story — hook → proof → proof → aspiration. The viewer scrolls through a mini-journey.

**Show, don't tell**: Slides 2-4 use product screenshot mockups (Asana boards, Slack messages, Figma flows) to make the claim concrete. You see what "handles admin work" actually looks like.

**Text lives in the image**: LinkedIn carousel cards carry their own headline + subhead inside the image. The post-level text is the hook; the slides are self-contained.

**Consistent visual system**: Cream background, black headline font, product mockups in bordered cards, "Try Claude" footer on every slide. Recognizable across swipes.

**Connected copy across slides**: "Skip the admin tasks..." (slide 2) → "and the status reports" (slide 3) is one sentence split across two cards, rewarding the swipe.

### What to note for YARNNN

**We don't have product screenshots yet**: Claude shows real integrations (Asana, Slack, Figma). We'd need to mock up what a YARNNN agent output looks like — a delivered Slack digest, a Monday morning email, a Notion update.

**Their copy formula maps to ours**: Their "[Product] handles [pain list]" = our "Your Monday morning work — already done." Their "Skip the admin tasks" = our "Connect your tools. Assign the work. It delivers." The voice is different (theirs is smoother/softer, ours is blunter/more declarative) but the structure matches.

**Carousel is phase 2-3 for us**: High production cost per slide. Start with single-image or text-based ads on Reddit to test copy, then graduate to carousel on LinkedIn once we have 3-4 proof mockups.

**Priority asset to create**: A mock of what a YARNNN agent delivery actually looks like (Slack message with a weekly digest, email summary, Notion page update). This becomes the reusable proof screenshot across channels.
