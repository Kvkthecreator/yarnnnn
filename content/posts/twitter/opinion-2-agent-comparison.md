**Voice:** Kevin

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
There are three ways to build an AI agent right now.

Each makes a fundamentally different bet about what actually matters. And the bet you make determines whether your AI gets smarter — or starts from zero every session. 🧵

**Tweet 2 (Claude Code)**
Claude Code: the Tool Model.

Session-based. Stateless. You open it, do great work, close the terminal. Tomorrow? It's never met you.

CLAUDE.md is clever — but it's a static file you maintain manually. It doesn't learn. Session 51 is essentially identical to session 1.

**Tweet 3 (OpenClaw)**
OpenClaw: the Agent Model.

Persistent identity (SOUL.md), accumulated memory (MEMORY.md), continuous awareness via heartbeats. The agent "lives" in your workspace. Gets smarter automatically.

But: heartbeats burn compute 24/7. One agent per workspace. 20 specialized tasks = 20 always-on processes.

**Tweet 4 (YARNNN)**
YARNNN: the Deliverable Model.

Network of sleeping specialists. Each deliverable has its own memory, instructions, sources, schedule. Wakes on trigger, executes with full accumulated context, goes back to sleep.

20 deliverables = 20 specialized agents. Zero idle cost.

**Tweet 5 (The real test)**
The model that wins isn't the smartest or most autonomous.

It's the one that knows your work best after 90 days.

Claude Code: no, by design.
OpenClaw: yes, at continuous cost.
YARNNN: yes, per specialist, at zero idle cost.

Which architecture would you bet on?
