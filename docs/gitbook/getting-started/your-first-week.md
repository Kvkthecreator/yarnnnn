# Your First Week

YARNNN gets more useful the more is in it. Here's the shortest path from empty workspace to one that's actually working for you.

## Day 1 — Put your existing material in

The fastest way to make the workspace useful is to stop it being empty.

**Files → right-click the canvas → Add Files.** Drop in the documents you already work from: PDFs, Word documents, notes, markdown. PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD, and ZIP are accepted.

Uploaded documents land in **Downloads** and become searchable. Your lanes can read them straight away.

## Day 2 — Work a real problem in Chat

Not a test question — an actual one. The point of grounding is that the answer starts from your material, and you can only tell whether that's working on something you know the answer to.

Try a Researcher lane for something that needs sources, or a Thinker lane for a decision you're stuck on.

## Day 3 — Settle something

This is the habit that makes the difference.

When a conversation reaches a conclusion, ask the lane to write it down as a file. A decision note, a brief, a summary of what you worked out.

Why it matters: a conversation you don't settle is gone in a week. A file you settled is something every future lane can read, every artifact can be built from, and every teammate can see.

## Day 4 — Make the artifact

Open Studio. Build the thing the thinking was for — a doc, a deck, a page.

Use the bound lane to draft from what you settled, then shape it yourself. The lane is faster at the first 60%; you're better at the last 40%.

## Day 5 — Correct something

Find a file with something wrong in it. Fix it.

Then notice what that did: everything made from it afterwards starts from the corrected version. You won't be re-explaining that thing again.

This is the compounding, and it's the one property you can only feel by doing it.

## Day 6 — Connect your other AI

Add `https://mcp.yarnnn.com` as a connector in ChatGPT or Claude — see [the setup guide](../integrations/mcp-connector.md).

Then use it the way it's meant to be used: while working in ChatGPT, tell it to remember something. Next day, in Claude, ask about it.

## Day 7 — Bring someone in

If you're working with anyone, invite them: **Workspace Settings → Access → invite by email**.

Their chat lanes stay private. What they settle into Files is shared. Every revision carries their name.

## The habits that make it work

| Habit | Why |
|---|---|
| Settle conversations into files | Thinking that doesn't land is thinking you'll redo |
| Fix files rather than re-prompting | A correction to a file is permanent; a correction in a chat is not |
| Upload rather than paste | Uploaded material is searchable by every lane, forever |
| Check `Get Info` when something surprises you | The history usually explains it |
| Set a budget early | So spend is a decision rather than a discovery — see [Usage](../plans/usage.md) |

## What not to expect yet

- **Scheduled background work.** Connected platforms can be authorised and scoped, but automatic pulling on a cadence is not running yet.
- **Freddie as a chat partner.** He's ambient — you see his work in the record, not in a conversation window.
