# Template: Twitter Thread

**Max tweets:** 6 (prefer 4-5)
**Voice:** Kevin (default) or YARNNN brand for thesis threads
**Read before drafting:** `_voice/kevin-voice.md` or `_voice/yarnnn-brand-voice.md`

---

## Structure

**Tweet 1 — Hook (under 200 chars)**
Sharp, standalone, makes someone stop scrolling. Tease the insight without giving it away. Must work as a standalone tweet if it doesn't go viral enough for people to click through.

**Tweet 2 — Problem**
Expand the pain. Make it visceral and specific. The reader should think "that's exactly my experience."

**Tweet 3 — Insight**
Introduce the named concept. This is where the original thinking lives. Frame it as a contribution to discourse, not a product pitch.

**Tweet 4 — Evidence**
Proof, example, analogy, data point. Make the insight concrete. "17,830 stars in 24 hours" or "I watched it write a client update from Slack messages I'd forgotten about."

**Tweet 5 — Implication**
What this means for the reader. Why should they care? Where is this heading?

**Tweet 6 — Close (optional)**
Engagement prompt: a question that invites replies. Soft link to yarnnn.com ONLY if natural and relevant. No "check out our product" energy.

## Rules

- No hashtags unless something is genuinely trending
- End with a question, not a CTA
- Each tweet must make sense if read in isolation (people will quote-tweet individual ones)
- Link thread back to at least one named concept from `_voice/named-concepts.md`
- If Pillar 1 (Category Authority): keep it category-level, NOT consultant-specific

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Hook works as standalone tweet?
- [ ] At least one named concept introduced or reinforced?
- [ ] No internal jargon?
- [ ] No marketing language ("game-changing," "revolutionary," etc.)?
- [ ] Correct voice guide followed?
- [ ] Would someone share this even without knowing about yarnnn?
