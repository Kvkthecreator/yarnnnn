**Date:** 2026-06-10 (sixth session capture, same day)
**Hat:** B (external-developer surface — research capture + ratified resolution).
The resolution and the hero block below were **ratified by the operator

**Origin:** the marketing-refactor kickoff (full-site scope, noun-pass-first —

**Method:** bottom-up web research (June 2026) on how the bounded-operation

---

## 1. Findings (evidence-grounded)

1. **"Solopreneur" consolidated as the 2026 umbrella person-noun.** People who
   once said freelancer / consultant / creator / independent now self-describe
   as solopreneurs; the term has real volume and a standardized meaning
   (one-person business, systems + IP + repeatable revenue, no employees).
   **But it structurally excludes half the psychographic**: the double-jobber
   (employed person running a side operation), the in-company
   consequence-bearer (A&R, partnerships, PM), and traders — who self-describe
   as *"I trade my own account" / "I manage my own portfolio"*
   (independence/control language, never "solopreneur").
2. **The possessive-run pattern generalizes where the person-noun fragments.**
   Across every vertical the self-identification is *"I run a ___"* — a
   newsletter, a shop, my own book, a one-person business, a pipeline. The
   person-noun varies or is absent; **the verb is universal.** This matches
   the psychographic frame's own first qualifier ("something that's **theirs
   to run**") — the architecture vocabulary and the layman vocabulary agree
   at the verb and diverge at the noun.
3. **The felt-pain phrase has no organic search presence.** 2026
   solopreneur/AI discourse is selling AI stacks, not complaining about
   resets. The pain ("I keep starting over") is therefore a **recognition
   hook** (they nod on contact), not a **search hook** (they don't type it).
   SEO load falls on category/channel terms; the identity hook does
   conversion work on-page.

## 2. The resolution (ratified)

**There is no single layman noun, and that is the answer, not a failure: the
noun varies by vertical; the VERB is universal.**

- **Umbrella surfaces key on the run-relationship** — "the work you run" —
  merging the verb-frame with the canon one-liner's nouns (*work*,
  *compounds*; ESSENCE v14 "the workspace where work is cumulative").
  "Thing" was considered and rejected as placeholder-grade (operator).
- **Vertical surfaces use the natural operation-noun** (newsletter ·
  portfolio · shop · pipeline · book of business) — per-vertical surface copy
  over the shared spine, exactly the option usp-spine §6 Q3 anticipated.
- **"Solopreneur"** = channel/SEO vocabulary where it earns volume; never the
  identity claim. **"Operator"** = internal vocabulary only (as THESIS's
  internal/external split always suspected).

### The ratified umbrella hero (B-structure, A-diction — operator-merged)

> **The work you run shouldn't reset.**
>
> YARNNN is the workspace where it compounds. Agents you own produce it.
> Corrections carry forward. A judgment seat answers for what ships — even
> when you're not there.
>
> *(a newsletter · a portfolio · a shop · a pipeline · a book of business)*

Why this satisfies the standing disciplines: *run* = the layman verb (finding
2); *work* = the grounded canon noun; *shouldn't reset* = the wince in plain
words (recognition hook per finding 3); the subhead carries the **mechanism
immediately** (owned · corrections-carry-forward · judgment seat — per ESSENCE
v14's retired-seeds rule: no bare capability claim without its mechanism);
*"even when you're not there"* = the non-presence qualifier; the chips do the
per-vertical noun work.
