# Twitter Thread: Messy Middle

**Post:** Opinion #3 — The Messy Middle of AI Work
**Voice:** Kevin
**Link:** yarnnn.com/blog/the-messy-middle-of-ai-work

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
The AI discourse is stuck between two extremes:

"AI is just a tool" (incumbent cope)
"AI replaces everything" (doomer fantasy)

Both are wrong. They're both skipping an entire era of work we'll live in for the next decade. 🧵

**Tweet 2 (Three eras)**
Three eras of AI work:

Era 1: Human-driven. AI as autocomplete. Where most people are.
Era 2: Human + AI. AI executes, humans judge. The messy middle.
Era 3: Agent-to-Agent. Fully autonomous. Where the hype points.

Almost nobody is seriously building for Era 2. That's the gap.

**Tweet 3 (Adoption curve uncertainty)**
What adoption curve are we even on?

Electricity: 40 years before factories redesigned.
Internet: spectacular crash, then quiet transformation.
Containerization: a simple metal box took 20 years to reorganize global logistics.

Trying to predict the timeline is dangerous. Not thinking about it is worse.

**Tweet 4 (A2A infrastructure gap)**
Here's the uncomfortable part about Era 3 (Agent-to-Agent):

The models might be ready. The world isn't.

No agent economy. No trust/verification layer. No governance rails. GAAP, HIPAA, SOC 2 don't contemplate autonomous agent actors.

These aren't edge cases. They're the foundation.

**Tweet 5 (Close)**
The question I can't stop asking:

Are we so focused on making agents that can do the work that we've ignored building the world those agents would operate in?

Is infrastructure — not intelligence — the real bottleneck?

I don't have the answer. But it's the question that matters most.
