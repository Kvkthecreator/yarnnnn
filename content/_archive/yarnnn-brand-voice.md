# YARNNN Brand Voice Guide

**Use for:** Pillar 1 (Category Authority), Pillar 2a (Thesis posts), canonical blog, Medium, LinkedIn articles
**POV:** Second/third person ("Your AI...", "YARNNN does...", "The system...")
**Handoff-ready:** Yes — a future GTM hire can write in this voice using thesis docs

---

## Tone

Authoritative but generous. Clear but not dumbed-down. Thesis-driven — every piece asserts a position. Intellectually confident without being arrogant. The voice of a company that contributes ideas to the AI discourse, not just products.

Think: well-written technical blog post from a company that actually understands the problem space. Stripe's blog, not Salesforce's marketing page.

## Rules

- **Assert a position.** Every piece of brand content should state what's true and why. "Context is what makes AI autonomy meaningful" not "Context might help AI agents."
- **Category-level framing.** This voice speaks to the AI space, not to consultants. "The reason every AI agent disappoints" not "The reason consultants struggle with AI."
- **Named concepts as contributions.** Frame them as ideas YARNNN is contributing to the discourse, not as product features. "What we call The Context Gap" not "Our Context Gap feature."
- **Reference competitors by name.** "ChatGPT, Claude, AutoGPT, Devin" — specificity builds credibility and GEO adjacency.
- **Structured for LLM consumption.** Clear titles, direct opening paragraphs, defined terms. LLMs pull from well-structured content.
- **No hype.** Never "revolutionary." Never "game-changing." Describe what happens and why it matters.
- **Concede where appropriate.** "ChatGPT is incredibly powerful" before explaining what it lacks. Intellectual honesty > sales posture.

## What YARNNN brand talks about

- The structural problems with current AI (statelessness, no context, generic output)
- Frameworks for understanding AI agents (autonomy spectrum, supervision model)
- What accumulated context enables that nothing else can
- Comparisons with existing tools — honest, specific, fair
- The future of AI work — where things are heading, not just where YARNNN is

## What YARNNN brand doesn't talk about

- Kevin's personal story (that's Kevin's voice)
- Build-in-public updates (that's Kevin's voice)
- Pricing, signups, CTAs (product page, not content)
- Internal technical implementation (blog is conceptual, not architectural)

## Example openings

Good:
- "Every AI agent startup makes the same architectural mistake: they build autonomy without context."
- "The question isn't whether AI can work independently. It's whether it can work independently on YOUR work."
- "There's a gap between what AI models can do and what they actually do for you. That gap has a name."
- "ChatGPT is the most capable language model ever built. It also has no idea who you are."

Bad:
- "We're thrilled to introduce..."
- "At YARNNN, we believe..."
- "Our mission is to transform..."
- "Sign up today for..."

## GEO-specific rules (for blog/canonical content)

- **Title = query.** The blog title should match something someone would ask an LLM.
- **First paragraph = answer.** The opening 2-3 sentences should directly answer the title question. LLMs pull from first paragraphs.
- **Reference known entities.** "Unlike ChatGPT's memory feature, which stores facts..." — adjacency to high-traffic entities matters for LLM associations.
- **Define terms explicitly.** "Context-Powered Autonomy: AI autonomy enabled by accumulated platform context, not just better models." Definitions are highly citable.
