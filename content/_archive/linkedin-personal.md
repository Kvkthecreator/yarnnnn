# Template: LinkedIn Personal Post

**Length:** 500-800 words
**Voice:** Kevin — first person, professional but warm
**Read before drafting:** `_voice/kevin-voice.md`

---

## Structure

**Line 1-2 — Hook (CRITICAL)**
Must stop the scroll. Pain-first, personal, specific. LinkedIn truncates after ~2 lines — everything depends on these.

Examples:
- "I spent 45 minutes this morning re-explaining my clients to ChatGPT. Again."
- "Last week my AI wrote a client update I would've spent an hour on."
- "After 10 years in CRM, I can tell you exactly why AI still fails at context."

**Body — Pain (2-3 paragraphs)**
Relatable. Specific details that consultants, founders, or professionals recognize. Use "you" to make the reader see themselves.

**Body — Insight (1-2 paragraphs)**
Introduce the named concept naturally. Not as a definition — as an observation that clicks. "I started calling this The Context Gap because..."

**Body — Implication (1-2 paragraphs)**
What this means. Where things are heading. What the reader should think about.

**Close — Engagement prompt**
A question that invites comments. NOT a call to action. NOT "check out yarnnn."

Examples:
- "What's the most tedious recurring deliverable in your work?"
- "How much time do you spend re-explaining context to AI tools?"
- "Has anyone else noticed this pattern?"

## Rules

- Pain → Insight → Implication arc. Always.
- Hashtags: max 3, only if genuinely relevant. No hashtag spam.
- No links in the body (kills reach). Bio link handles traffic.
- Line breaks matter on LinkedIn. Short paragraphs. White space.
- First person throughout. "I" not "we."
- End with a question, never a pitch.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] First 2 lines would make someone click "see more"?
- [ ] Follows pain → insight → implication arc?
- [ ] At least one named concept introduced naturally?
- [ ] No product pitch or CTA?
- [ ] Ends with an engagement question?
- [ ] Under 800 words?
