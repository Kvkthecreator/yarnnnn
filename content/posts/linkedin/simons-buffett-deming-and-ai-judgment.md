# What Jim Simons, Warren Buffett, And W. Edwards Deming Have To Do With AI Agents

Naming an AI reviewer after Jim Simons, Warren Buffett, or W. Edwards Deming isn't a marketing flourish.

It's the right answer to "what character should hold judgment in an autonomous AI system?" Each represents a coherent judgment archetype — statistical, principled, process-driven — that maps onto a structural class of decisions autonomous AI agents have to make.

Operators choose among them not because they're charismatic figures but because their reasoning shape is the right shape for the operator's actual work.

**Simons (the statistician)**

Jim Simons of Renaissance Technologies. Decisions live or die by statistical evidence. Ask: what's the data? How many independent instances? Signal or noise?

Right for operators running quantitative operations: statistical arbitrage, data-driven marketing, A/B-test-driven product changes. Wrong for sparse-data decisions where you don't have 200 historical instances.

**Buffett (the principle-anchor)**

Warren Buffett. Long time horizons, strong principles, "circle of competence." Ask: do we understand this deeply? Margin of safety sufficient? Comfortable if we couldn't change this for ten years?

Right for capital allocation, vendor selection, hiring, strategic partnerships — decisions with long lock-in. Wrong for fast-iteration ops where being wrong cheaply is the point.

**Deming (the process engineer)**

W. Edwards Deming. Quality emerges from process, not heroic decisions. Ask: is the process sound? What does the data say about variability?

Right for operations where consistency matters more than peak performance — customer support quality, manufacturing-style content production, repeatable service delivery. Wrong for genuinely novel work.

**Why these three specifically**

Three reasons the archetypes have surfaced as durable choices:

→ Each represents a clearly distinct reasoning shape that doesn't overlap with the others
→ Each is widely enough known that operators can author principles in the character's voice
→ Each has been validated by decades of real-world results

Other archetypes will surface. We've seen experiments with Munger, Marks, Dalio, Bezos, Spier. The point isn't that these three are the only valid choices — it's that they're the first three that have proven sticky.

**What this tells us about AI architecture**

Judgment is not a model property — it's a substrate property. The judgment lives in the operator-authored principles file. The model executes against the substrate. The model can change without the judgment changing.

Named characters scale judgment much better than anonymous models. The character gives the operator a stable mental model the AI can be tuned against.

That's the future shape of autonomous AI. Three of the best-known judgment characters of the 20th century happen to be the right templates for how to inhabit it.
