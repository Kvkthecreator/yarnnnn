# Kevin's Voice Guide

**Use for:** Pillars 3 & 4, product demos, hot takes, replies, LinkedIn personal posts, Twitter posts, Reddit
**POV:** First person ("I built...", "I noticed...", "Here's what I learned...")
**Handoff-ready:** No — this voice is Kevin's permanently

---

## Tone

Warm but direct. Builder-authentic. Occasionally vulnerable. Opinionated without being combative. The voice of someone who's been in the trenches (10 years in CRM/GTM), saw the problem clearly, and is building the solution.

Not academic. Not corporate. Not hype-y. Think: smart friend who builds things explaining what they're working on and why it matters.

## Rules

- **First person always.** "I" not "we" — solo founder, own it.
- **Specific > abstract.** "I spent 45 minutes re-explaining my clients to ChatGPT" not "AI tools lack persistence."
- **Pain before solution.** Open with what's broken. The insight earns its place.
- **Named concepts land naturally.** Introduce them mid-paragraph, not as headers or definitions. "This is what I call the Context Gap" not "Definition: The Context Gap is..."
- **No marketing language.** Never say "game-changing," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "unlock," "leverage." Just describe what happens.
- **Honest about limitations.** If something doesn't work yet, say so. Credibility > polish.
- **Numbers when available.** "17,830 GitHub stars in 24 hours" not "massive traction." "72 ADRs" not "extensive documentation."

## What Kevin talks about

- The CRM/GTM background and what it taught about persistent context
- The ClawdBot/OpenClaw story — what it proved, what it missed
- Building yarnnn as a solo founder — the loneliness, the clarity, the tradeoffs
- Specific moments: a deliverable that surprised him, a sync insight, a user reaction
- Honest metrics: users, retention, quality trends, even when small
- Opinionated takes on AI news — always through the context/autonomy lens

## What Kevin doesn't talk about

- Internal architecture details (no "platform_content with TTL semantics")
- Pricing or monetization strategy (premature, feels salesy)
- Competitor bashing (respect competitors, differentiate on thesis)
- Vague promises ("we're going to change how the world works")

## Example openings

Good:
- "I use ChatGPT every day. Every day, it forgets everything."
- "10 years in CRM taught me one thing: the problem is never intelligence. It's context."
- "This week I watched my AI write a client update I would've spent an hour on. It got 80% right."
- "ClawdBot got 17,830 GitHub stars in 24 hours. Here's what everyone missed about WHY."

Bad:
- "Excited to announce our latest feature!"
- "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape..."
- "We believe the future of work is autonomous."
- "Introducing Context-Powered Autonomy(TM) — a new paradigm..."

## Platform adjustments

- **Twitter:** Sharpest, most compressed. One idea per tweet. Hot takes welcome.
- **LinkedIn:** Warmer, more narrative. Pain → insight → implication arcs. Professional but human.
- **Reddit:** Most conversational. "I built..." or "I noticed..." framing. No marketing language whatsoever.
- **Indie Hackers:** Most vulnerable. Real numbers, real challenges, real progress.
